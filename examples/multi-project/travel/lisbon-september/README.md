## Rough Itinerary

Not day-by-day yet. Just clusters of things we want to do.

**Lisbon Core (5-6 days)**
- Alfama neighborhood — Castelo de São Jorge, Feira da Ladra flea market (Tuesday/Saturday)
- Belém day — Jerónimos Monastery, Pastéis de Belém (the original pastel de nata), Torre de Belém, MAAT museum
- Bairro Alto / Chiado — bookshops, Café A Brasileira, evening fado in a small venue
- LX Factory — converted warehouse district, good for lunch and browsing
- Time Out Market for one meal (touristy but the stalls are actually good vendors)
- At least one evening just walking the Tagus waterfront

**Day Trips**
- **Sintra** (1 day) — Pena Palace, Quinta da Regaleira, lunch in town. Go early to beat crowds.
- **Porto** (1-2 nights?) — 3-hour train from Lisbon. Port wine cellars, Livraria Lello, Ribeira district. Debating whether to do this as an overnight or skip it this trip and save it for a future visit. Alex wants to go, I'm on the fence about the logistics.

**Rest Days**
- At least 2 days with no plan. Sleep in, find a neighborhood cafe, read, wander. We always overplan trips and regret it.

## Research List

**Neighborhoods to explore on foot:**
- Mouraria — less touristy than Alfama, good street art, multicultural food scene
- Graça — miradouros (viewpoints), Feira da Ladra flea market
- Estrela — Basilica da Estrela, Jardim da Estrela, quieter residential feel

**Restaurants / Food:**
- Cervejaria Ramiro — seafood, locals say go for the prawns
- Tasca do Chico — fado and petiscos in Bairro Alto
- Manteigaria — rival pastel de nata spot to Pastéis de Belém
- Solar dos Presuntos — old-school Portuguese, apparently a splurge-worthy dinner
- Find a good place for bifana (pork sandwich) — seems like every local has a different favorite spot
