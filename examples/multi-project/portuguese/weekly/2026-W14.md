## Notes

- 2026-03-28 — Mariana said my "ão" pronunciation is improving noticeably. She could tell I'd been drilling it with Pimsleur. "lh" still needs work — she gave me a list of words to practice: "trabalho," "filho," "olho," "velho," "melhor." Going to add these as a separate Anki deck with audio.
- 2026-03-28 — Discovered *PortuguesePod101* through a Reddit thread. The grammar breakdowns are clearer than the textbook. The episode on pretérito perfeito vs. imperfeito finally made the distinction click: perfeito is a completed action ("I ate"), imperfeito is ongoing/habitual ("I used to eat" / "I was eating").
- 2026-03-28 — Realized I should start building a [[../../travel/lisbon-september/README|Lisbon trip]] phrase sheet — not just textbook phrases but practical stuff like reading menus, understanding metro announcements, asking "is this the right tram."
