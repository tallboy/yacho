## Overview

Started learning European Portuguese in January 2026. Primary motivation: the [[../travel/lisbon-september/README|Lisbon trip in September]] with Alex. Secondary motivation: always wanted to learn a Romance language and Portuguese is underrated — 260 million speakers, incredible music, and the pronunciation is genuinely interesting (it sounds nothing like Spanish once you tune your ear to it).

**Current level:** High A1. Can introduce myself, order food and drinks, ask basic directions, handle simple transactions. Still slow and need people to repeat things. Reading is ahead of listening comprehension.

**Target:** B1 conversational by September. Should be able to navigate daily life in Lisbon without defaulting to English — restaurants, transit, asking for help, basic small talk.

## Progress Notes

**What's working:**
- Pimsleur is great for drilling pronunciation and building automatic responses. The spaced repetition of phrases means I don't have to think about common constructions anymore.
- Anki for vocabulary fills in the gaps Pimsleur leaves — Pimsleur doesn't teach you "weather" or "apartment" but those come up in real conversations.
- Mariana (italki tutor) is excellent. She speaks slowly, corrects gently, and gives homework that's actually useful (she had me write a paragraph about my weekend and we reviewed it together).

**What's hard:**
- **Pronunciation of nasals.** The "ão" sound (like in "não" or "estação") is getting better but "ãe" (like in "mãe") still trips me up. It's a completely different mouth position than anything in English.
- **"lh" sound.** As in "trabalho" or "elho." It's like the "lli" in "million" but the tongue position is different. Mariana says I'm making it too much like a "y" sound.
- **Listening comprehension.** European Portuguese swallows vowels in a way Brazilian Portuguese doesn't. "Desculpe" sounds like "dshkulp." I can read it fine but hearing it at native speed is another story.
- **Verb conjugations.** Portuguese has more tenses than I expected. The subjunctive is everywhere and I keep confusing pretérito perfeito with imperfeito.
